Project Focus

Built Around One Analytical Direction

The goal is not simply to display customer metrics. The system is designed to move from understanding the customer base, to diagnosing how customers engage and evaluate the platform, and finally to prioritising retention and value outcomes.

Why this matters for fintech teams

Product and CRM teams need to answer connected questions, not isolated ones. A customer segment is only useful when it can be linked to platform behaviour. Behavioural patterns are only useful when they help explain advocacy, churn risk, or customer value.

Using the COFINFAD dataset, this project turns customer-level data into a structured decision-support workflow for business users who need interpretable, interactive analysis rather than static reporting.

What the system is built to support

  • Identify who the customers are and how they differ across segments and locations.
  • Diagnose which usage and experience indicators actually matter.
  • Connect behavioural and service patterns to retention risk and customer lifetime value.
  • Support business prioritisation through interactive exploration and predictive modelling.
Storyline

The Storyline Drives the Entire Website

There is no separate storyboard page in the final website. The storyline is presented here as the main analytical path of the project and the logic that connects all three modules.

Progressive analytical journey

The system starts with customer structure, moves into behavioural and experience diagnosis, and ends with retention and customer value analysis. Each stage prepares the context for the next one.

1

Customer Structure

The first step establishes the analytical foundation. Users explore segment composition, product adoption, transaction behaviour, and geographic patterns to understand what the customer base looks like.

  • Who are our customers?
  • How are they distributed?
  • What early behavioural patterns appear?
2

Behaviour & Experience Diagnosis

The second step explains how customers interact with the platform and how they evaluate that experience. This layer distinguishes stronger signals from weaker ones and surfaces where friction emerges.

  • Which engagement signals matter?
  • How does satisfaction relate to advocacy?
  • Where does service friction appear?
3

Retention Risk & Customer Value

The final step moves from explanation to prioritisation. Users examine churn context, value concentration, and predictive CLV modelling to identify which customers matter most for retention and growth.

  • Who is more valuable?
  • Which groups need attention?
  • How well can value be predicted?
Modules

Three Modules with Three Different Jobs

The modules are not equal summaries of the same dataset. Each one plays a distinct role in the overall logic of the system: foundation, diagnosis, and prioritisation.

MODULE 01

Customer Overview & Segmentation

Role: Foundation layer

This module introduces the customer base through segment structure, product adoption, transaction behaviour, and city-level pattern discovery. It is the entry point for the rest of the system.

  • Interactive customer explorer table
  • Segment distribution across age groups
  • Transaction behaviour by age and segment
  • Geographic behavioural clustering
MODULE 02

Customer Engagement & Experience

Role: Diagnostic bridge

This module sits between structure and outcomes. It tests how product ownership relates to platform usage, how satisfaction translates into advocacy, and where complaints and support patterns reveal service friction.

  • Feature usage breadth by product ownership
  • Login frequency comparison
  • NPS linked to satisfaction and usage
  • Complaint and support burden analysis
MODULE 03

Retention Risk & Customer Value

Role: Prioritisation and modelling layer

This module extends the analysis to business outcomes. It combines churn context, geographic value patterns, and predictive modelling of customer lifetime value using Linear Regression and Random Forest.

  • CLV distribution by segment
  • Churn comparison across segments
  • Geographic value analysis
  • Predicted vs actual CLV evaluation
